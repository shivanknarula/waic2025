Huawei's Ascend 384 Super Node: A Game-Changer in High-Performance AI Computing

By: A report on Huawei's innovation at WAIC 2025

At the World Artificial Intelligence Conference (WAIC) 2025, Huawei unveiled its groundbreaking Ascend 384 Super Node, a high-performance AI computing solution designed to significantly advance large-scale AI model training and inference. Officially known as CloudMatrix 384, it integrates 384 of Huawei's latest Ascend 910C AI processors across 16 racks, including 12 compute and four networking racks.

A core innovation is its proprietary, all-optical mesh network interconnect, replacing traditional Ethernet. This boosts communication bandwidth by 15 times and reduces single-hop latency from 2 microseconds to 200 nanoseconds, crucial for seamless data exchange in large-scale AI tasks. The system delivers an impressive 300 petaflops of raw computational power (BF16 precision), positioning it as a direct rival to Nvidia's GB200 NVL72 system. It also boasts 48 terabytes of high-bandwidth memory (HBM) and over 1229 TB/s of total memory bandwidth.

Optimized for advanced AI workloads, its peer-to-peer architecture is especially powerful for Mixture-of-Experts (MoE) models, popular for large language models. This unveiling challenges global dominance, signifying China's ambition for independence in AI technology amidst US trade restrictions. It demonstrates how architectural design can compensate for manufacturing limitations, laying a powerful foundation for the future of AI.

The Groundbreaking R1 Reasoning Model: Logic and Explainability

DeepSeek's flagship presentation revolved around the DeepSeek-R1 model, a groundbreaking large language model designed to excel in complex reasoning tasks.

  • Advanced Reasoning Capabilities: DeepSeek-R1 is specifically engineered for logical inference, mathematical problem-solving, and real-time decision-making. It demonstrates superior performance across various benchmarks related to mathematics, programming, and general logic, rivaling and in some cases surpassing leading models from global competitors.
  • Explainable AI: A key differentiator of R1 is its ability to demonstrate its reasoning process. Unlike many "black box" AI models, R1 can show how it arrived at a conclusion, making its output more transparent and trustworthy. This "thinking out loud" capability, often presented within <think> and </think> tags in its chat interface, is crucial for applications requiring explainability, such as scientific research, legal analysis, and complex decision support.
  • Reinforcement Learning and Hybrid Training: DeepSeek-R1's development journey is unique, stemming from an initial reinforcement learning (RL) only approach (DeepSeek-R1-Zero) and then refined through a hybrid approach combining RL with supervised fine-tuning (SFT). This combination addresses challenges like readability and language mixing, resulting in more polished and practical responses while retaining strong reasoning capabilities.
  • Cost Efficiency and Open Source: DeepSeek-R1 stands out for its impressive performance at a significantly lower operational cost compared to many leading models. This cost-efficiency is achieved partly through an optimized Mixture-of-Experts (MoE) architecture, where only a fraction of its 671 billion parameters are activated per query. Crucially, DeepSeek-R1 is open-source under the MIT License, allowing free commercial and academic use, and enabling developers and researchers worldwide to explore, modify, and deploy it. This open-source strategy actively promotes collaboration and accelerates AI innovation across the community.
